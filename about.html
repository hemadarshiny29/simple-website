ABOUT FLIPKART

History of Flipkart (India’s E-commerce Pioneer) Flipkart is one of India's biggest e-commerce companies, and its journey is a remarkable story of entrepreneurship and growth. 🏁 Founding and Early Years (2007–2009) Founded: October 2007 Founders: Sachin Bansal and Binny Bansal (not related) Background: Both were former Amazon employees. Headquarters: Initially based in Bangalore, India. 🛒 First Product Sold: A book—Leaving Microsoft to Change the World Flipkart started as an online bookstore. 🚀 Expansion (2010–2014) 2010: Started selling electronics like mobile phones and laptops. 2011: Introduced its own supply chain system and launched its logistics arm, eKart. 2012: Introduced digital wallet – PayZippy (later discontinued). 2014: Acquired fashion e-commerce platforms Myntra and later Jabong (2016). 📱 Flipkart also launched its mobile app during this period, boosting online shopping on smartphones. Innovation & Technology AI & Machine Learning: Flipkart uses AI/ML for product recommendations, fraud detection, and personalized shopping experiences. Voice Search & Vernacular Languages: Introduced features supporting regional languages like Hindi, Tamil, and Kannada to tap into India’s non-English-speaking population. Flipkart Labs (2022): Focuses on Web3, blockchain, metaverse, and NFTs, exploring the future of digital commerce. Camera-based shopping: Uses AR (Augmented Reality) to let users try products like furniture and fashion before purchase. 🛠️ Infrastructure & Logistics eKart (since 2009): Flipkart’s logistics arm, one of the largest in India, delivers to 90%+ of Indian PIN codes. Flipkart Fulfilment Centers: Hundreds of warehouses across India that help with faster delivery and inventory management. Flipkart Quick (2020): A 90-minute hyperlocal delivery service, primarily for groceries and daily needs.

Funding and Valuation Flipkart raised funds from major investors like Tiger Global, Accel Partners, SoftBank, Naspers, and DST Global. By 2015, Flipkart had become India’s most valued startup, surpassing $15 billion in valuation. 🔄 Challenges & Competition (2015–2017) Faced tough competition from Amazon India (launched in 2013). Struggled with profitability and leadership changes. Focused more on fashion, electronics, and private labels (like MarQ). 🛍️ Walmart Acquisition (2018) In May 2018, Walmart acquired a 77% stake in Flipkart for $16 billion, the world’s largest e-commerce deal at the time. Sachin Bansal exited the company after the deal; Binny Bansal stepped down later in the year. 🧠 Recent Developments (2019–Present) Flipkart Wholesale: Launched in 2020 to support B2B businesses. Flipkart Health+, Flipkart Quick (hyperlocal delivery), and more niche services introduced. Expanded to grocery, furniture, and insurance sectors. Partnerships with local sellers and major brands to strengthen its marketplace. 📈 Today Parent Company: Walmart Key Competitors: Amazon India, Reliance JioMart, Tata Neu Popular Services: Flipkart Plus (loyalty program), SuperCoins, Flipkart Pay Later 🏆 Impact Flipkart transformed how Indians shop online. Played a major role in shaping India’s e-commerce ecosystem. Empowered small sellers and created thousands of jobs. Controversies & Criticism Big Billion Day Flop (2014): Massive online sale faced technical crashes and price inflation allegations, leading to public backlash and CEO apology. FDI Norms Violation (2019): Investigated for possibly bypassing India’s foreign investment rules for e-commerce. Antitrust Allegations: Accused (alongside Amazon) of favoring select sellers and offering deep discounts, harming small retailers. 🌱 Contribution to Startup Ecosystem Flipkart Mafia: Similar to the “PayPal Mafia” in the US, many ex-Flipkart employees have launched their own startups like: Razorpay, Udaan, Ninjacart, Cure.fit, Groww, Cred These companies have shaped India's tech and startup ecosystem significantly. 🧠 Leadership After Walmart Kalyan Krishnamurthy became the CEO post-Walmart acquisition. Focus has shifted toward: Operational efficiency Strategic partnerships (like with Adani for warehousing) Rural & Tier 2/3 market penetration 📊 Market Position As of 2025, Flipkart holds over 30–35% of the Indian e-commerce market. Strong in fashion (via Myntra), electronics, and mobile phones. Expanding in grocery and healthcare through Flipkart Health+ and Supermart.
